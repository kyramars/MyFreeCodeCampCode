Massimo Vignelli

"If you can design one thing, you can design everything."

Here's a timeline of Massimo Vignelli's life:

  • 1931 - Born in Milan, Italy
  • 1957 - Designs hand blown glass tumblers
  • 1963 - Designs the Pneumatici Pirelli advertisement
  • 1964 - Designs stacking dinnerware which receives the Compoasso d'Oro Award for Good Design
  • 1967 - Designs the American Airlines logo, which endured for over 40 years
  • 1970 - Designs the signs for the New York City Subway system
  • 1977 - Designs the interior of NYC's Saint Peter's Church
  • 1980 - Introduces a new wave of "Austrian Architecture"
  • 1984 - Designs the Unfinished Modern typeface
  • 1985 - Designs the handkerchief armless chair
  • 1995 - Designs "United Colors of Benetton"
  • 1995 - Designs silver jewelry for San Lorenzo
  • 2008 - Redesigns the New York subway map
  • 2010 - Releases The Vignelli Canon
  • 2014 - Died in New York City, New York at the age of 83

"The life of a designer is a life of fight. Fight against the ugliness. Just like a doctor fights against disease. For us, the visual disease is what we have around, and what we try to do is cure it somehow with design."
